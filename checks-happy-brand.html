01 — Brand Foundation

The Brand Idea

Checks Happy sits at the intersection of craft and organisation. It exists for the people who care deeply about their work and deserve tools that match that energy. The name is warm and optimistic. The brand should be too.

The Rainbow as a System, Not a Symbol

The 70s rainbow is the inspiration, not the output. Rather than using a literal rainbow graphic, the layered colour principle becomes a design system: colours stack, overlap, and build depth. Joy is expressed through warmth, not decoration.

08 — Usage Rules

Do and Don't

The brand system only works if it's applied consistently. These rules protect the visual language across every touchpoint.

Do
+Use Playfair Display for all display headings, hero text, and brand name treatments.
+Use the warm brown dark mode base, not grey or black.
+Let the arc motif appear as depth and layering in backgrounds and illustrations.
+Use orange as the single primary action colour across both modes.
+Keep teal as a supporting accent, used sparingly for contrast and success states.
+Use italic Playfair for warmth and personality in headlines.
Don't
×Use a literal 70s rainbow stripe as a graphic element. The reference is systemic, not decorative.
×Use Inter, Roboto, or system fonts. They undermine the brand character entirely.
×Use cold greys or pure black in the dark mode palette.
×Use more than two typefaces on any single page or screen.
×Use the brand name in all caps. It is always Checks Happy, never CHECKS HAPPY.
×Use teal as a primary action colour. It supports, it does not lead.
